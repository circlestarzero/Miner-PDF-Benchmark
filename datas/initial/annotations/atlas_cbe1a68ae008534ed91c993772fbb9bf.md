EARTHQUAKES AND VOLCANOES

$\mathrm{R}$ esearchers believe that the Earth's crust is cracked into huge pieces that fit together like a giant puzzle. The cracked sections, called tectonic plates, are supported by the oozing, soft rocks of the mantle beneath the Earth's crust. The unstable borders between the plates are known as rings of fire. These areas are danger zones for both volcanoes and earthquakes. A volcano is an opening in the Earth's crust which allows redhot magma (molten rock) from the mantle to escape onto the surface of the Earth. An earthquake is a shaking of the ground caused by movements of rocks beneath the Earth's surface.
